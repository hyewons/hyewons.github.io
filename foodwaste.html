UW Food Rescue

A desktop web platform that connects UW dining facilities with non-profit agencies for food recovery efforts.

Client: University of Washington
Duration: Sept 2017 - Present
Role: User Research, UX/UI Design
Tools: Illustrator, Figma

Every year, 33 million tons of "good" food is wasted in the U.S.

Food waste is a growing problem globally, with an increasing number of people struggling with food insecurity. Admist this growing problem the University of Washington prides itself on its commitment to sustainability. Backed by the UW’s award winning Campus Sustainability Fund, the project proposes to build and design a website platform to pair-up UW food waste with local non-profit agencies in need. While a few facilities on campus partner with organizations such as Food Lifeline to reduce food waste, many others cannot connect with agencies in need. The website would facilitate the logistics and transportation of food waste from UW without having so many food products go to compost.

Evolution of the project goal

Due to logistical issues, strict food safety rules and lack of resources of the local non-profit agencies, our project scope and primary user group changed constantly. Through this experience, I was able to learn how project goal can unexpectedly change due to realistic constraints.

Exploratory Research

To gain a comprehensive understanding of the current food waste/donation practices, we interviewed our three stakeholders: UW dining facility, local food agencies, and student groups. The rich insights were helpful for gathering insights for each stakeholder's needs and goals and setting the stage for low-fidelity prototypes.

Usability Testing

With the low fidelity prototypes, we went out to test with our main users including student groups and food agencies. We aim to test if 1) every information is digestabile, 2) interface is simple and easy enough to use for non tech-savvy users and 3) features meet their needs.

Conducting usability testing with food shelter manager

Future Direction

We plan to iterate on the prototype while continuing the usability studies simultaneously. When we finalize with the prototype, we will be developing fully responsive website with the developers and launch pilot test with few stakeholders. Ultimately, we want to make this as an open-source platform where other universities could lean on to reduce food waste around the country.
